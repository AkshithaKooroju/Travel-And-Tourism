WEST BENGAL

KOLKATA

4.8/5

Kolkata, India's second biggest city, is a perpetually ongoing festival of human existence, concurrently luxurious and squalid, refined and frantic, pointedly futuristic, while beautifully in decay.

DARJEELING

4.8/5

The Darjeeling Himalayan Railway established back in 1881 is conferred the title of World Heritage Status by UNESCO. The train begins its journey from the plains and rises to over 2000 metres above sea level, offering breathtaking views of the mountains as it chugs along. Darjeeling is also famous for it's tea plantations with over 86 tea estates responsible for producing the worldwide famous 'Darjeeling Tea'.

MIRIK

4.9/5

Nestled amidst the picturesque hills of Darjeeling district in West Bengal, Mirik is a charming hill station known for its serene beauty and tranquil ambiance. Located at an altitude of 1,495 meters above sea level, Mirik is surrounded by lush green forests, rolling tea gardens.

KALIMPONG

4.8/5

Kalimpong is a virgin hill station in West Bengal renowned for its panoramic valley views, Buddhists monasteries and churches and Tibetan handicrafts etc.

MURSHIDABAD

4.6/5

From the Nawabs of the pre-British era to the Lords from England, Murshidabad has seen history from its core. This small tourist haven in West Bengal serves as a place that manages to combine the beauty of the past with the beliefs of the present.

KURSEONG

4.6/5

Also known as the 'Land of White Orchids', Kurseong is a hill station located close to Darjeeling famous for its waterfalls, temples and Buddhist gompas apart from the excellent view that the place provides.
